For 3 monthes, I engaged in a good parcel of my free time in making and learning from Javascript Challenges on Sololearn. On the +300 JS wins badge mission, I confess that I used way more than my free time to achieve this. I put all my intense focused energy on it - and I even was in doubt to accomplish it when there were still 80 challenges in 3 hours to go - but I hit the target goal in 2 hours for my surprise... In the next day (July 15th, 2019), after I have achieved this memorable number of JS wins, I decided to make something dope with the production of this code (By the way, not only with quiz challenges live a programmer). But the fun fact part comes up to say that I did not play any challenges during this entire next day and yet all the pending challenges from the epic day made me score 100 wins with an entire day of inactivity (it counts more than 400 challenges counting up draw, losses (6 of them), rejected and pending ones. Okay... it was 90 wins and I have to complete the rest up to 100 in the end of the day. But 90 wins without doing "nothing" looks like a very good XPs passive income. (=

After a few days when I have finished this celebration code and then started advertising my custom-made +300 badge, I was surprised when I scrolled my profile and find out how hackable can be these challenge daily performance graphics, and I realized that my results have been altered due to Sololearn server instability (or hacking). Yeah! It was a good idea that I took screenshots and I have a solid proof about this unfortunate event:

Click on the images for zooming if needed!

The blue ellipse is a concrete proof that is the exact the same graphic I am comparing. Take a note that the last columns are different because I made more challenges during that same day.

I sent an email to the SL support reporting the issue and I received in the next day this email below as feedback, but it made clear that they could not help me out apparently.

Right after that, I try again with new arguments on why they should help me like the fact that I was celebrating and advertising for something that I did not possess any longer in my graph screenshots - multimedia. One of these "minor differences" was 13 wins away from my original result. I sent more 2 emails after that with screenshots attached and code and I did not receive further feedback about it since then.

It is not my style to make a decision of announcing delicate technical issues like this, but I waited for a solution more than a week and tried my best to solve it and make it as private as possible, but - since the support could not fix it, then I decided to write about it publicly. That's the reason I am writing about it. I learned a lot with Sololearn App and I thankful for everything they provided me, but I can not tolerate urfairness with my own endeveours at all and let it pass in blank. I am just making it clear.

As you may have perceived it already, this second mission day was not that pleasant moment as I had in the first day (both coding and challenges day). Coding because I faced issues with my code structure (although I inserted new cool things around) and SL servers are awkward these days. And in challenges day because I had to sacrifice even more than my tolerated time for trying again to rescue my result with these challenges in one single day. If I fought and I did once, then I can reach +300 wins for one more time and be in hope that my graphs do not be hacked again. That what I thought and I did as practical measure. Not only I accomplished the goal, but also overtake my previous record on Javascript wins in a day. From 302 wins (not 289) to my new record of 329 Javascript wins . (At the moment that I am writing this I still have my 329 wins with no hacking gladly). The second day was a mission to get my badge back since SL did not repair my issue. But everybody who check it out will notice that I indeed reached twice this badge.

The second day was harder than the first one especially in the last hours it seemed like there were no more JS warriors to be challenged with. I remember that I sent JS challenges for about 1 hour straight without stopping with no response at all and I had less than 300 wins to go and less than 1:30 hours left.
